Course reader Stanford's undergraduate market design (ECON 136). Reader focuses on three areas: the design of matching algorithms to solve assignment problems, with applications to school choice, entry-level labor markets, and kidney exchanges; the design of auctions to solve general resource allocation problems, with applications to the sale of natural resources, financial assets, radio spectrum, and advertising; and the design of platforms and exchanges, with applications to internet markets. Emphasis on connecting economic theory to practical applications. [Thumbnail image credit](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=3033208)
